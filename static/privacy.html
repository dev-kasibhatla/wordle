Privacy

Simple by design.

The public deployment is built to keep state short-lived and to avoid collecting more than it needs.

What the service sees

  • Requests sent to the API.
  • Basic network metadata needed to protect uptime, including IP-based rate limiting.
  • Operational logs for debugging and abuse detection.

What not to send

  • No secrets.
  • No personal data.
  • No production traffic you cannot afford to expose.

How to avoid shared risk

  • Run your own copy from the public repository if you need strict control.
  • Review the Docker and deployment scripts before you ship.
  • Treat the public instance as a public instance.
